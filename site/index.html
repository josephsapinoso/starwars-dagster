The census

Here they are: all 82 — every character the saga's own database keeps a record on, from Luke Skywalker to Ratts Tyerel. A galaxy of billions, on file: 82 rows.

The measuring

Line them up by height and the census spans a four-fold range — from Yoda at 66 cm to Yarael Poof, 264 cm of mostly Quermian neck.

One X-wing pilot, Arvel Crynyd, was never measured at all.

The weighing

Mass is rude — and broken. Jabba Desilijic Tiure weighs 1,358 kg, eight and a half times the runner-up, General Grievous. He single-handedly bends this chart onto a log scale.

And 23 of the 82 never stepped on a scale: only the 59 weighed characters ride the axis.

The hometowns

Where is everybody from? Two zip codes. Naboo claims 11 characters and Tatooine 10 — together 21 of 82, a quarter of the census from one swamp kingdom and one desert.

Five more list their homeworld as “unknown.” The galaxy has paperwork problems.

The cameos

Stack the census by screen persistence and the truth comes out: 42 of 82 — more than half — appear in exactly one film. The galaxy far, far away is mostly one-scene wonders.

The witnesses

Climb the stack and only three characters remain in all six films: C-3PO, R2-D2 — and Obi-Wan Kenobi, who had to become a ghost to keep his attendance.

The famous claim that “only the droids saw it all” fails the data check. Ben counts.

The pilots

So who actually flew this galaxy? Only 19 of 82 are ever listed at a starship's controls. Sized by ships flown, the census crowns Obi-Wan Kenobi — five starships — the man who insisted that flying is for droids.

The handoff

That's the census: measured, weighed, located, counted. The machinery that counted it — five raw SWAPI pulls, one DuckDB warehouse, three transforms, one report — is waiting below.
